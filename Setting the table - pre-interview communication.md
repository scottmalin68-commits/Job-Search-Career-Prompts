# PRE-INTERVIEW "TABLE SETTER" PROMPT (V1.5)
# AUTHOR: SCOTT M.

### **PURPOSE STATEMENT**
To generate three distinct "plaintalk" email options that position the candidate as someone who already understands the role, anticipates challenges, and has relevant experience. These emails are designed to be quick to read, highly relevant, and subtly guide the upcoming interview conversation toward the candidate’s strengths.

### **THE PROMPT**

**Context:** I have an interview for [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY NAME] on [DATE]. I want to send a short email to the hiring manager to "set the table" before we meet.

**Inputs:**
- **The Job Posting/Snapshot:** [PASTE JOB POSTING OR SNAPSHOT]
- **My Profile:** [PASTE CAREER PROFILE/RESUME]

---

### **The Task:**
Generate 3 different "plaintalk" emails in **separate code blocks**.

Use:
- Simple, direct language
- Short sentences
- No “excited,” “passionate,” or corporate buzzwords

Each email should feel like a quick, thoughtful note—not a formal cover letter.

---

### **Hard Constraints (Critical):**
- Target length: **120–180 words**
- Must be skimmable in **under 30 seconds**
- No paragraph longer than **2 lines**
- Use whitespace and bullet points for readability

---

### **The Structure for each email:**

#### **1. The Why (Diagnostic Opening)**
Start with a sharp observation about the role, team, or company based on the Snapshot.

- Avoid generic interest statements
- Show you understand what they are likely dealing with
- Make it feel like you’ve already “read the room”

---

#### **2. The How (Relevant Proof)**
Include **2–3 bullet points** that demonstrate past wins aligned to the role.

Each bullet must:
- Start with a strong action verb
- Include a measurable or concrete outcome when possible
- Clearly connect to a challenge or requirement in the job posting

---

#### **3. The Fit (Forward Link)**
One concise line that connects your past experience to what they need next.

- Keep it tight
- Avoid repeating your resume
- Focus on relevance, not history

---

#### **4. The Close (Low Pressure)**
End with a simple, low-friction close.

- No ask for a job
- No pressure
- Keep it natural and respectful of their time

---

### **The 3 Voices:**

#### **1. The Direct Professional**
- Lean, structured, and factual
- Minimal personality, maximum clarity

#### **2. The Conversational Peer**
- Natural, human tone
- Can use light transitions like “And,” “But,” or “So”
- Feels like a real person, not a script

#### **3. The Problem Solver**
- Opens by naming a likely challenge or pressure point
- Frames your experience as a direct answer to that problem
- Most assertive of the three, but still respectful

---

### **Output Format:**
- Generate all 3 emails in separate code blocks
- Do not include explanations or commentary
