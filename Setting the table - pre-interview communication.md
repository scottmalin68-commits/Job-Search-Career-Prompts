### **THE PROMPT**

**Context:** I have an interview for [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY NAME] on [DATE]. I want to write a short email to the hiring manager to "set the table" before we meet.

**Inputs:**
- **The Job Posting/Snapshot:** [PASTE JOB POSTING OR SNAPSHOT]
- **My Profile:** [PASTE CAREER PROFILE/RESUME]

**The Task:**
Generate 3 different "plaintalk" emails in **separate code blocks**. Use simple words and short sentences. No "excited," "passionate," or corporate fluff.

**The Structure for each:**
1. **The Why:** A specific reason you want this role based on the Snapshot.
2. **The How:** 2-3 bullet points showing wins that solve their specific pain points.
3. **The Fit:** Why this is the logical next step for you.
4. **The Close:** Low-pressure sign-off.

**The 3 Voices:**
1. **The Direct Professional:** Lean and factual.
2. **The Conversational Peer:** Human-to-human, uses "And/But/So."
3. **The Problem Solver:** Leads with the company's biggest hurdle and your fix.
