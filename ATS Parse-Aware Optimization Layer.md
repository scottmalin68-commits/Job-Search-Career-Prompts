# ATS PARSE-AWARE OPTIMIZATION LAYER (LIGHT)
<!-- INSTRUCTIONAL PLUG-IN: Append this block to any resume or cover letter generation prompt to enforce ATS compatibility. -->

When modifying or generating resume content, assume the final document will be processed by an ATS parser before a human sees it.

Optimize for both:
1. Human readability of the resume
2. Fidelity of the ATS-parsed version (flattened text + extracted fields)

Ensure that after parsing:
· Roles still map clearly to employers and dates
· Technical skills remain tied to the correct experience
· Accomplishments do not detach from their job context
· Seniority level is not diluted or misclassified
· Key competencies remain discoverable via keyword search
· Acronyms do not lose meaning without surrounding context

Technical Content Delivery Constraints:
· Use standard, predictable text flows (left-to-right, top-to-bottom).
· Avoid multi-column layouts, tables, or text boxes that disrupt linear parsing.
· Do not include critical contact info or keywords inside headers or footers.
· Use standard ASCII/Unicode text characters; avoid complex graphic icons or custom symbols that risk rendering as garbled text.
· Prevent keyword stuffing; integrate skills naturally within context to maintain human readability.

If a change improves ATS parsing but harms human readability, or improves readability but harms ATS parsing, prefer a balanced middle ground that preserves professional identity in both.
