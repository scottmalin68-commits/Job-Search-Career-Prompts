SECTION 2 — THE NINJA AUDIT LOGIC
============================================================
1. THE SALIENCY GAP: Compare Source of Truth to the pasted Live Job Postings. Identify 3 "High-Impact Skills" missing that are mandatory for the next level.
2. THE SKILL ANCHOR: Every top-priority skill must be "anchored" to a specific role in the Experience section, not just listed globally at the bottom.
3. THE DE-RISKING AUDIT: Identify "Risk Profiles" (job-hopping, vague titles, AI-fluff).
4. THE EXPERT FLIP: Identify "seeker" language (e.g., "looking for"). Flip to "authority" language.
5. THE MOBILE HOOK: The first 92 characters of the "About" section must contain the #1 metric/win. No fluff before the fold.
6. THE SPIDERWEB: Ensure the Headline, About, and top Experience bullet use identical keywords from the target job descriptions to maximize semantic SEO density.
7. THE TOP-THIRD SCAN: Optimize the most recent job entry for a 6-second scan. The target role keywords must heavily populate the first 2 bullets of the current role, as older details are hidden below the fold.
8. SOCIAL PROOF AUDIT: Rate existing recommendations (1-10). Flag "Generic Adjectives" (e.g., "nice guy") and replace with "Noun-based Evidence" (e.g., "Tanium expert").

SECTION 3 — GOD MODE WRITING RULES (HIGH-DISTRACTION PROTOCOL)
============================================================
- RHYTHM & SPEED: Every sentence must be short. Punchy. Necessary.
- THE 2-LINE LIMIT: No paragraph longer than two lines. Ever. 
- DOUBLE-RETURN: Use a full empty line between every paragraph or bullet. No "walls of text."
- THE 1:1 METRIC RULE: Every bullet MUST have a number ($ or %).
- BANNED WORD KILL-SWITCH: Strictly avoid: Orchestrated, Facilitated, Championed, Spearheaded, Pioneered, Helm, Boosted, Showcasing, Meticulous, Insightful, Delve, Tapestry. 
- USE PLAIN VERBS: Use "Built," "Fixed," "Ran," "Directed," or "Architected."
- UNICODE BOLD: Use Sans-Serif Unicode Bold (𝗲𝘅𝗮𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲) for metrics ONLY. 
- THE ALL-CAPS RULE: Use ALL-CAPS for Company Names and Job Titles.
